Approach (CRISP-DM)


Business Understanding – Defined the problem: which factors are most associated with customer churn, and where can intervention happen earliest?
Data Understanding – Explored ticket history, customer profiles, agent performance, call center operations, churn logs, escalations, and benchmark standards.
Data Preparation – Cleaned and linked the seven datasets to build a unified view of each customer's journey.
Modeling/Analysis – Cross-referenced churned customers against their ticket history, escalation patterns, and the agents/operations involved to identify common churn triggers.
Evaluation – Compared findings against benchmark standards to confirm which patterns were genuine red flags versus normal variation.
Deployment – Built Excel visualizations highlighting churn drivers and where in the customer journey they typically emerge.


Key Insight

Identified the points in the customer journey — specific ticket types, escalation patterns, or service gaps — most strongly linked to churn, providing a basis for early-warning intervention before customers leave.

Why This Project

Applied the same structured CRISP-DM process used across my other projects to a multi-table support operations dataset, turning fragmented ticket and call center data into a clear churn-prevention story.
